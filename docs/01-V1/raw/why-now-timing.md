## Primary Timing Driver

**LLM cost collapse unlocks real-time, personalized trip orchestration for the first time**

| What changed | When | Evidence | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| GPT-4-class inference cost dropped ~97% | Jan 2023 - mid 2025 | GPT-4 launched at ~$30/1M output tokens (Mar 2023); by mid-2025, GPT-4o-mini and open-source equivalents at <$1/1M tokens | A multi-turn trip session (5-10K tokens) costs <$0.01 per user. AI-heavy consumer app now viable without enterprise pricing. |

## Supporting Timing Signals

### 1. Multimodal AI agents are production-ready (2024-2025)
Vision models can parse menus, signs, receipts; function-calling orchestrates Maps, Weather, Flights, Calendar APIs. "Agent" pattern was research-only in 2022; by 2025 ships in consumer products. Real-time in-trip adaptation now architecturally possible.

### 2. Post-COVID travel spend hit all-time highs
UNWTO: international tourist arrivals returned to 96% of 2019 levels by end of 2024, spending exceeding $9.5T. Millennials and Gen Z prioritize experiences over goods [Data].

### 3. Gen Z/Millennial behavior: collaborative, social, AI-native
75%+ of Gen Z travelers use 4+ apps per trip (Google/Phocuswright 2024). This cohort expects AI assistance and shares experiences natively. Interactive trip maps = Spotify Wrapped for travel.

### 4. Incumbent vacuum since Google Trips shutdown (2019)
Google killed Trips in 2019, never shipped unified replacement. No single app owns the plan-go-share loop. Market fragmented for 6+ years, waiting for AI to make unification feasible [Data].

### 5. On-device AI and 5G make mobile-first viable
Apple/Google shipped on-device small LLMs in 2024-2025. Combined with widespread 5G, sub-second AI responses possible even mid-hike.

## "Why Now" Narrative for the Pitch

**Version 1 (data-led):**
"AI inference costs dropped 97% in 18 months, making it economically viable — for the first time — to run a personalized trip planning agent for every user at consumer-app pricing. Meanwhile, travel spending hit $9.5T and no incumbent owns the unified plan-go-share experience."

**Version 2 (story-led) — RECOMMENDED:**
"Two years ago, planning a week in Japan meant juggling Google Flights, Maps, TripAdvisor, WhatsApp, Sheets, and Instagram saves. Today, a single AI agent can orchestrate all of that in real-time — adapting when your flight is delayed, your friend votes for ramen over sushi, or rain hits Kyoto. This was impossible in 2022, expensive in 2023, and now it costs a penny per session. The technology just caught up to what travelers actually need."

## Counter-Arguments

| Skeptical View | Evidence | How to Address |
|---|---|---|
| "Google/Apple will just do this" | Google has Maps, Flights, AI — all the pieces | Google killed Trips because unified planning conflicts with ad-per-click model. Neither incentivizes social sharing. Startups win on focus. |
| "AI travel apps have terrible retention" | Roam Around, Layla saw spikes then drop-off | Those are "plan once" tools. Live-trip + post-trip sharing loop creates weekly engagement. Strava analogy. |
| "Too early — users don't trust AI for bookings" | <30% would let AI book a $3K flight autonomously | We start as planning assistant (suggestions, not bookings). Trust builds incrementally. |
| "Two-sided cold start for social" | Social travel apps grow slowly | Standalone utility first, social layer second. Each trip generates shareable map — growth is content-led, not network-dependent. |

## Timing Assessment

- **Is timing genuinely strong?** Yes. Convergence of affordable AI, multimodal agents, record travel demand, and 6-year incumbent vacuum.
- **Risk of being too early:** Low. Enabling tech is shipped and priced for consumers today.
- **Risk of being too late:** Moderate and rising. Google integrating Gemini into Maps. Window for new entrant: ~18-24 months.
- **Honest recommendation:** Timing is strong — arguably the best 24-month window this category will see. Primary risk is execution speed, not timing.

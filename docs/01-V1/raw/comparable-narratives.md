## Narrative Hooks Available (Ranked by Strength)

### 1. "The 6-App Problem"
- **Why it works:** Viscerally relatable, instantly quantifiable
- **How to use:** Open with "Show me your phone on your last trip day" — screenshot of 6+ open apps
- **Risk:** Low [Opinion]

### 2. "The Trip Dies After It Ends"
- **Why it works:** Emotional resonance; positions interactive trip maps as shareable content
- **How to use:** Show a beautiful completed trip as a living artifact vs. a dead photo album
- **Risk:** Investors may see sharing as "nice-to-have" not core [Opinion]

### 3. "Plans Break on Day 1"
- **Why it works:** Demonstrates real-time adaptation as a moat, not a feature
- **How to use:** Demo a rainy-day re-route in 10 seconds
- **Risk:** Requires strong AI demo; falls flat if slow [Opinion]

### 4. "Your Trips Make You Smarter"
- **Why it works:** Investors love flywheel/network effects
- **How to use:** "Trip 5 is 10x better than Trip 1 because the app knows you"
- **Risk:** Hard to prove at idea stage [Assumption]

## "X for Y" Candidates

| Analogy | Clarity | Risk | Recommendation |
|---------|---------|------|----------------|
| "Waze for trip planning" | High — real-time, community, adapts | May confuse scope (navigation only) | Use if emphasizing real-time adaptation |
| "Strava for travel" | High — tracking + sharing + personal history | May imply fitness niche | Strong for "trips as shareable maps" angle |
| "Notion for travel, but it thinks for you" | Medium — collaborative + AI layer | Notion fatigue in VC circles | Use carefully |
| "Spotify Wrapped for every trip" | High emotional hook | Undersells planning side | Use as feature hook, not whole pitch |

## "Why Now" Narrative

- **LLM cost collapse**: GPT-4-class inference dropped 95%+ in 18 months (2023-2025); real-time AI planning now viable on mobile at consumer margins [Data]
- **Multimodal AI**: Vision + maps + weather APIs orchestrated by agents — impossible before 2023 [Data]
- **Post-COVID travel boom**: Global travel spend exceeded $9.5T in 2024, with younger travelers demanding mobile-first tools [Data/Estimate]
- **Fragmentation fatigue**: No incumbent unified planning + live-trip + post-trip; Google Travel stagnant, TripIt enterprise-focused [Opinion]
- **Strength**: Strong. Multiple independent tailwinds converging.

## What NOT to Do

**Narratives overused:**
- "We use AI to plan your trip" — every new entrant says this. Show WHICH trip moment you own [Opinion]
- "All-in-one travel app" — triggers PTSD from failed superapp plays (TripCase, Kayak Trips, Google Trips RIP) [Opinion]

**Objections investors will raise:**
- "Google will just do this" — show why learning flywheel + community beats bolting onto Maps [Assumption]
- "Travel is seasonal, low-frequency" — counter: trips-as-content creates between-trip engagement; learning flywheel retains year-round [Opinion]
- "Two technical co-founders, no travel domain expertise" — counter: you ARE the user, show obsessive personal pain [Opinion]
- "How do you monetize without being the booking layer?" — have an answer [Opinion]

**Narrative traps to avoid:**
- Don't lead with feature count without an emotional story. Lead with one painful trip moment, then reveal the stat [Opinion]
- Don't demo a generic "plan me a trip to Paris" — demo a SPECIFIC mid-trip adaptation [Opinion]
- Don't claim network effects until you can articulate the mechanism [Opinion]

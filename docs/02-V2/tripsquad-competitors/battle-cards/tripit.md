# Battle Card: TripIt

*Skill: startup-competitors | Generated: 2026-05-21*

> **[Knowledge-Based -- verify independently]**

## Who They Are

The original travel organizer app, founded 2006, acquired by Concur (now SAP) in 2011. Parses booking confirmation emails to automatically build itineraries. Dominant in business travel. Pro at $49/yr.

## Their Strengths

- Magic email parsing -- forward confirmations, get an organized itinerary
- Deep flight data (real-time alerts, seat maps, fare refund tracking)
- SAP Concur enterprise distribution (millions of business travelers)
- Trusted, established brand
- Strong in business travel segment

## Their Weaknesses

- **Business travel DNA** -- not designed for leisure group trips
- **No collaborative planning** -- sharing is read-only
- **No group decision features** -- no voting, no collaborative editing
- **Aging UX** -- hasn't had a major redesign in years
- **Innovation stalled** under SAP corporate ownership
- **No photo features, no expense splitting, no place discovery**
- **Reactive, not proactive** -- organizes bookings after they're made, doesn't help plan

## How to Win Against TripIt

1. **Different use case entirely**: "TripIt organizes your business flights. TripSquad helps your friend group plan a trip to Barcelona together."
2. **Planning vs organizing**: "TripIt starts after you've booked everything. TripSquad helps your group decide what to book in the first place."
3. **Group-first vs solo**: "TripIt shares your itinerary. TripSquad builds your group's itinerary together."

## When TripIt Wins Over You

- Business travelers (expense reports, corporate travel policies)
- Frequent solo flyers who want flight tracking
- Users who primarily need booking confirmation aggregation

## Key Vulnerability to Exploit

TripIt is **stuck in SAP's corporate roadmap**. Innovation is slow, the focus is enterprise, and the leisure/group market is clearly not their priority. They won't pivot to serve group leisure travelers.

## Churn Signals

- Users finding TripIt outdated compared to newer planning tools
- Leisure travelers who outgrew TripIt's business-travel focus

---

## Red Flags

- TripIt's brand recognition could cause confusion ("isn't TripIt already for trip planning?")

## Yellow Flags

- SAP could invest heavily if they decided to enter the leisure market (unlikely but possible)

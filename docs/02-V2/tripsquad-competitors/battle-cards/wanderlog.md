# Battle Card: Wanderlog

*Skill: startup-competitors | Generated: 2026-05-21*

> **[Knowledge-Based -- verify independently]**

## Who They Are

AI-powered trip planner with best-in-class itinerary UX. Founded 2019, SF-based, ~15-20 person team. Strong SEO moat through city guide content. Cross-platform (web + iOS + Android). ~$3.5M raised [Estimate].

## Their Strengths

- Best itinerary builder in the market (drag-and-drop, map-integrated, time estimates)
- AI trip generation creates full itineraries from a prompt
- Massive SEO content library (city guides rank #1-3 on Google for many destinations)
- Cross-platform: web, iOS, Android
- Generous free tier drives adoption
- Google Sheets import for existing plans
- Hotel and restaurant booking integration

## Their Weaknesses

- **No group decision tools** -- no voting, no polling, no shortlisting. Collaboration = "everyone can edit the same doc"
- **No photo features** -- planning only, nothing for during or after the trip
- **No expense splitting** -- groups still need Splitwise alongside
- **Per-user pricing** -- in a group of 6, convincing everyone to go Pro is hard
- **Solo-traveler DNA** -- product was designed for individual planners; group features were added later
- **No public trip pages** -- trips are private by default, no social/shareable component

## How to Win Against Wanderlog

1. **Lead with the group decision problem**: "Planning a trip with friends? Wanderlog lets everyone edit, but TripSquad lets everyone vote. No more 47-message WhatsApp debates about which restaurant."
2. **Emphasize the full lifecycle**: "Wanderlog stops when the trip starts. TripSquad covers planning, the trip itself (photo sync), and shareable memories after."
3. **Price advantage for groups**: "Wanderlog Pro costs $48/person/year. TripSquad is $39 for the organizer -- everyone else is free."
4. **Highlight the organizer pain point**: "You're the one who always plans the trips. You shouldn't have to convince 6 friends to download AND pay for an app."

## When Wanderlog Wins Over You

- Solo travelers or couples (TripSquad's group features are irrelevant)
- Users who want AI trip generation from scratch
- Android users or groups with mixed devices
- Users who need web access for desktop planning
- Power planners who want deep itinerary features (time estimates, opening hours, drive times)

## Customer Objections & Responses

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Wanderlog already has collaboration" | "Collaboration isn't the same as group decisions. Can your group vote on which hotel to book in Wanderlog?" |
| "Wanderlog is free" | "So is TripSquad for everyone except the organizer. And you're already paying $48/yr if you use Wanderlog Pro." |
| "Wanderlog has AI trip planning" | "AI gives you a starting point. But your group still needs to agree on it. TripSquad is built for the messy group negotiation part." |

## Key Vulnerability to Exploit

Wanderlog's fundamental architecture is **individual-first, shared second**. Their collaboration model is "Google Docs for trips" -- everyone can edit, but there's no structure for group decision-making. Adding voting and shortlisting would require rethinking their core UX. This is TripSquad's biggest window.

## Churn Signals

- Users frustrated by "too many cooks" editing the same itinerary with no conflict resolution
- Groups that tried Wanderlog but reverted to WhatsApp for decision-making
- Users wanting to share trip memories (Wanderlog has no post-trip features)

---

## Red Flags

- Wanderlog could add voting features; their team is capable and fast-moving
- Their SEO moat means they own the top of the funnel for "trip planning" searches

## Yellow Flags

- Wanderlog's AI features are a strong draw for the initial planning phase

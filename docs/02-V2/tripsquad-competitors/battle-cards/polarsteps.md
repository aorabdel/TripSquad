# Battle Card: Polarsteps

*Skill: startup-competitors | Generated: 2026-05-21*

> **[Knowledge-Based -- verify independently]**

## Who They Are

Amsterdam-based travel tracking and journaling app. Automatically tracks trips via GPS and lets users create beautiful travel journals with photos. Revenue from subscriptions and printed photo books. ~30-40 person team, ~EUR 6M raised [Estimate]. 5M+ users [Estimate].

## Their Strengths

- Effortless GPS tracking (runs in background)
- Beautiful trip visualization on a map
- Strong photo integration with journaling
- Printed photo book revenue (high-margin, emotionally-driven purchases)
- Public trip sharing (others can follow along in real-time)
- Strong emotional product -- captures memories, not just logistics
- Good European market penetration

## Their Weaknesses

- **Zero pre-trip planning** -- no itinerary builder, no collaborative planning
- **No group decision-making** -- no voting, no shortlists, no shared itinerary
- **No expense splitting**
- **Individual-focused** -- tracks one person's trip, not a group's shared experience
- **Battery drain** from continuous GPS tracking
- **Requires app open/running** during travel for best tracking

## How to Win Against Polarsteps

1. **Own the full journey**: "Polarsteps is great for tracking where you've been. TripSquad helps your group decide where to go, coordinates the trip, then syncs everyone's photos together."
2. **Group vs individual**: "Polarsteps tracks YOUR trip. TripSquad plans and documents your GROUP's trip."
3. **Planning is the hard part**: "The hardest part of a group trip isn't recording it -- it's getting 6 people to agree on an itinerary."

## When Polarsteps Wins Over You

- Solo travelers documenting their journey
- Backpackers/long-term travelers wanting a travel journal
- Users who want printed photo books of their trips
- Adventure travelers focused on route tracking (hiking, road trips)

## Key Vulnerability to Exploit

Polarsteps has **no planning features at all**. They're a post-hoc documentation tool. Groups using Polarsteps still need a completely separate tool for planning. TripSquad can capture the planning phase and offer competitive post-trip features (photo sync, shareable pages).

## Churn Signals

- Users wishing Polarsteps had planning features
- Groups using Polarsteps alongside 2-3 other tools for the full trip workflow

---

## Red Flags

- Polarsteps' photo book business model is clever and could fund feature expansion into planning

## Yellow Flags

- Polarsteps' public trip pages are a feature TripSquad also plans -- they have a head start on this

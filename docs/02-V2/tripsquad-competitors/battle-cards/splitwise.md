# Battle Card: Splitwise

*Skill: startup-competitors | Generated: 2026-05-21*

> **[Knowledge-Based -- verify independently]**

## Who They Are

The dominant group expense splitting app. Founded 2011, ~50M+ downloads [Estimate]. "Splitwise" has become a verb among millennials for splitting costs. Small team (~20-30), modestly funded (~$2.2M). Revenue from Pro subscriptions ($49.99/yr).

## Their Strengths

- Category king -- unmatched brand recognition for group expenses
- Strong network effects (your friend group is already on Splitwise)
- Simple, reliable, does one thing well
- Cross-platform (web, iOS, Android)
- Deep group financial history creates stickiness

## Their Weaknesses

- **Not a trip planning tool** -- purely financial
- **No itinerary, maps, or place discovery**
- **No photo features**
- **No group decision-making beyond expense categories**
- **Subscription feels expensive for an expense tracker** -- $50/yr for what is essentially a calculator

## How to Win Against Splitwise

1. **Integration, not competition**: Position TripSquad as the planning layer that complements Splitwise, or build native expense splitting to make Splitwise unnecessary.
2. **"One less app"**: "Your group already juggles WhatsApp, Google Docs, and Splitwise for every trip. TripSquad handles planning and decisions. Add expense splitting and you eliminate one more app."
3. **Trip context**: "Splitwise knows you owe Sarah $47. TripSquad knows it was for the amazing seafood restaurant in Lisbon your group voted on together."

## When Splitwise Wins Over You

- Roommate expenses (non-travel use case)
- Groups that only need expense tracking, not planning
- Any group expense splitting outside of trips

## Key Vulnerability to Exploit

Splitwise has zero trip-specific features. They're a general-purpose expense tool. If TripSquad builds solid expense splitting within the trip context, Splitwise becomes redundant for travelers. The vulnerability is their **lack of vertical specialization** -- they can't add trip planning without becoming a completely different product.

## Churn Signals

- Users annoyed by ads in the free tier
- Users who find $50/yr expensive for basic expense math
- Travel-focused users wishing Splitwise connected to their itinerary

---

## Red Flags

- Splitwise's network effects are extremely strong -- getting groups to track expenses in a new app is hard
- Splitwise could partner with or acquire a planning tool

## Yellow Flags

- Building expense splitting well is non-trivial (currency conversion, unequal splits, debt simplification)
